Pre-Congress events

Towards the Plurinational Bayesian Congress 2023
During the year 2023, before meeting in person in Santiago del Estero, we will hold a virtual seminar with the participation of the most diverse profiles of the Plurinational Bayesian Community. Everything will be uploaded on our youtube channel. If you want to participate, send an email to bayesdelsur@gmail.com.
Seminar.
"Intersubjective agreements in contexts of uncertainty."

The seminar is an opportunity to discuss a wide range of topics, from practical, theoretical, and even philosophical. Among the topics to be discussed are all related to the Bayesian approach to statistics (not limited to):

  • • probabilistic modeling
  • • causal inference
  • • data science
  • • decision making
  • • probability theory
  • • statistics pedagogy
  • • epistemology and methodology
  • • probabilistic programming
  • • Bayesian workflow
  • • artificial intelligence

Other pre-congress activities:
Bayesian Modeling Course. From 28/11/2022 to 02/12/2022

This is an introductory course for people with no prior knowledge of statistics or data science. Familiarity with Python and Python libraries used in data analysis such as Numpy, matplotlib, Pandas, etc. is assumed.

Those who don't know Python but are familiar with other programming languages will also be able to take advantage of the course, although you may experience a little more friction.

Finally, those who are not interested in learning how to code for data analysis can still take advantage of some of the material to get a bird's-eye view of Bayesian methods.

Seminar Causal models and equity in machine learning. Start on 10/19/2022

The Department of Computing of the Faculty of Engineering (UBA) invites you to participate in this series of seminars.

The application of machine learning models for decision-making that affects people can help put an end to discriminatory episodes carried out by certain agents thanks to the automation of decision processes. However, it also raises ethical issues due to the possibility that algorithmic bias ends up reinforcing certain historical inequalities.

The study of the implications of the use of machine learning technologies in social contexts has given rise to an area of study known as Fairness, Accountability and Transparency (FAT). Through these seminars we seek to promote a space for discussion and debate on this topic of social relevance, and stimulate possible applications and studies related to it.
